One 2-year postdoctoral position in algebraic geometry. Deadline for application: November 13th 2025.

One 2-year postdoctoral position is available in the Algebraic Geometry group at the Department of Mathematics of the University of Milan.

Research topics

The position is funded by the university on the themes of the project “Birational geometry and moduli spaces of algebraic varieties and foliations” that is coordinated by Luca Tasin and myself.
We are looking for candidates whose research interests are related to one or more of the following topics (all intended in the widest possible sense): birational geometry, geometry of varieties with trivial canonical bundle, algebraic foliations, boundedness problems.

Starting date and duration of contract

The duration of the contract is 2 years.
The starting date will be no later than January 1st 2026 -- due to the nature of the funding supporting the position.
Should the starting date constitute an issue, do not hesitate to get in contact with us (even before the hiring process takes place).

Salary

The gross salary for the position is €28.283,94/year. That amount should roughly correspond, after taxes, to about €1600/month (over 13 monthly payments). Do notice that this is an estimate and the correct amount may depend from personal circumstances (family situation, etc.).
For those that, at the start of the contract, will have been working abroad (i.e., outside of Italy) for at least the previous 3 years, it will be possible to claim a tax benefit that should increase the salary to about €1900/month (over 13 monthly payments).

Research in AG at University of Milan

The Department of Mathematics at the University of Milan currently has a large research group in Algebraic Geometry currently featuring 14 permanent members, 2 assistant professors, 3 postdoctoral researchers, and 2 graduate students.
Research themes represented in the group cover a wide range of topics in modern AG: e.g., birational geometry, algebraic foliations, hyperkaehler varieties, stability conditions and moduli spaces, Hodge theory, derived categories, K-theory, and many more.
We organise a weekly seminar and reading groups during each semester, and we invite many academic visitors that make for a lively research and work environment.
More information on the group and its members are available here.

Application requirements

Holding a doctoral degree at the time of application is not a requirement for applying.
If not already held at the time of application, you will need to obtain your doctoral degree within 6 months of the date of posting of the call for the position, i.e., by April 13th 2026. Moreover, if you are in this situation, and you are still pursuing your PhD, you must be enrolled in the 3rd year of a doctoral program: this may seem a strange requirement, but it is due to how the law has been (not very smartly) written.

Those already holding a doctoral degree obtained outside of Italy will have to obtain the formal recognition of its equivalence to an Italian degre (equivalenza or equipollenza). Do not worry about that, we can help you with it.
The same rule apply to those that will obtain their doctoral degree by the deadline indicated above: in that case, the procedure for obtaining the formal recongition of the foreign degree will have to be initiated right after the awarding of the degree. Again, we can help with that.

For all the relevant information, about criteria and modality for application, please consult the official document of the call which is available at the official page of application.
