Abstracts
Ruadhai Dervan A Harder-Narasimhan filtration for unstable Fano varieties
Analogously to the Hitchin-Kobayashi correspondence, it is now known that a Fano variety admits a Kähler-Einstein metric if and only if it is "stable" in an algebro-geometric sense. We consider Fano varieties that do not admit a Kähler-Einstein metric. Motivated by the existence of the Harder-Narasihman filtration on an unstable vector bundle, we prove the existence of a "most destabilising" degeneration, with respect to a new stability notion. Our proof is analytic, rather than algebraic. This is joint work with Gábor Székelyhidi.
Julius Ross Variation of Moduli Spaces of Sheaves.
There are various notions of stability for sheaves (e.g. Mumford stability, Gieseker semistability) that are important in the production of projective moduli spaces. These notions usually depend non-trivially on a choice of ample line bundle, or Kaehler class and different choices give rise to different, but related, moduli spaces. In this talk I will discuss joint work with Daniel Greb and Matei Toma in which we introduce a notion of Gieseker-stability that depends on several polarisations. We use this to study the change in the moduli space of Gieseker-semistable sheaves on manifolds giving new results in dimensions at least three, and to study the notion of Gieseker-semistability for sheaves taken with respect to an irrational Kaehler class.
Hiromu Tanaka Purely log terminal threefolds with non-normal centres.
It is known that the centre of any plt pair is normal in characteristic zero. On the other hand, this property turns out to fail in positive characteristic. In this talk, I will explain how to construct such examples. This is a joint work with Paolo Cascini.
Andrea Fanelli Del Pezzo fibrations in positive characteristic.
Del Pezzo fibrations are possible outputs of a 3-fold MMP also in positive characterisitic. A natural question is whether geometrically non-normal del Pezzo surfaces can appear as generic fibre of such a fibration. This talk is based on a work in progress with S. Schröer.
Akihiro Kanemitsu Fano n-folds with ample vector bundles of rank n-2 whose adjoint bundles are trivial.
Let (X,E) be a pair of a Fano manifold X of dimension n and an ample vector bundle E of rank r with the condition that the first Chern class of X and that of E coincide.
Then the rank r is at most n+1 by Mori's theorem on rational curves, and Mukai conjectured the classification with r at most n, which was solved by Fujita, Peternell and Ye-Zhang.
Also the classification of pairs with $r=n-1$ was given by Peternell-Szurek-Wisniewski.
In this talk I will give the classification of pairs with r=n-2.
Takeru Fukuoka On the existence of almost Fano threefolds with del Pezzo fibrations.
We say that a smooth complex projective threefold is almost Fano if its anti-canonical divisor is nef and big but not ample. By Jahnke-Peternell-Radloff and Takeuchi, the numerical classification of almost Fano threefolds with del Pezzo fibration were given. Among the classification results, there exists exactly ten cases such that it was yet to be known whether these have an example or not. The main result of this talk shows that there exists examples for each of ten cases.
Tyler Kelly A Toric Orlov Theorem via Landau-Ginzburg Models.
Orlov's theorem defines and proves that any smooth Fano (resp. general type) hypersurface in projective n-space has a subcategory (resp. supercategory) in its bounded derived category of coherent sheaves that is a Fractional Calabi-Yau category. We prove this is the case for a certain class of toric complete intersections. The method to find this is by studying a Landau-Ginzburg model associated to the toric complete intersection and then using geometric invariant theory. We end with looking at a few motivating examples from studies involving mirror symmetry and rationality. This is joint work with David Favero (Alberta/KIAS).
Gabriele Di Cerbo Log birational boundedness of Calabi-Yau pairs.
I will discuss joint work with Roberto Svaldi on birational boundedness of Calabi-Yau pairs. Recent work in the minimal model program suggests that pairs with trivial log canonical class should satisfy some boundedness properties. I will show that 4-dimensional Calabi-Yau pairs which are not birational to a product are indeed log birationally bounded. This implies birational boundedness of elliptically fibered Calabi-Yau manifolds with a section, in dimension up to 5.
Yusuke Nakamura Rational points on log Fano threefolds over a finite field.
Esnault proved that smooth Fano varieties defined over a finite field have a rational point. In this talk, I will discuss the singular case. This is joint work with Yoshinori Gongyo and Hiromu Tanaka. We proved that log Fano threefolds with klt singularities defined over a finite field of characteristic larger than 5 have a rational point.
Sho Ejiri Weak positivity theorem and Iitaka's conjecture for 3-folds in positive characteristic.
Iitaka's conjecture is an important conjecture on algebraic fiber spaces which is studied by many mathematicians. It is known that the positivity of the direct image sheaf of a power of the relative canonical bundle is closely related to this conjecture. In this talk, we will discuss the weak positivity theorem for some special classes of algebraic fiber spaces in positive characteristic. We will then explain that this theorem is applied to prove Iitaka's conjecture in the case when the total space is a 3-fold and the characteristic is larger than 5. This is a joint work with Lei Zhang.
